## Capability

Examines a body of existing research — literature summaries, known findings, and field descriptions — to identify where knowledge is incomplete, methods are inadequate, or questions remain unanswered. Produces a structured gap analysis that serves as the foundation for hypothesis generation and research proposals.

## When to Use

- At the start of a research project to identify what hasn't been studied
- After a literature review to find opportunities for original contribution
- When scoping a research proposal to justify the research question

## Inputs

- Literature summaries or abstracts from the existing body of work
- Field description or research domain overview
- Known limitations or controversies in the field (optional)

## Outputs

A structured gap analysis covering:

- **Identified gaps** — specific areas where knowledge is incomplete
- **Underexplored methods** — methodological approaches not yet applied to this domain
- **Contradictions** — findings that conflict across studies
- **Opportunities** — where new research would have the most impact
- **Suggested research questions** — questions that directly address the identified gaps
