## Purpose

Reference document for Chicago citation formatting. Used by the citation extraction skill when Chicago style is specified.

## Notes-Bibliography System

### Footnote Format

#### Book
First note: Author First Last, *Title of Book* (Place: Publisher, Year), page.
Subsequent notes: Last, *Short Title*, page.

#### Journal Article
First note: Author First Last, "Title of Article," *Journal Title* volume, no. issue (Year): page.
Subsequent notes: Last, "Short Title," page.

### Bibliography Format

#### Book
Last, First. *Title of Book*. Place: Publisher, Year.

#### Journal Article
Last, First. "Title of Article." *Journal Title* volume, no. issue (Year): page-page. https://doi.org/xxxxx.

## Author-Date System

### In-Text Citations
- (Author Year, page)
- Author (Year, page)

### Reference List Format

#### Book
Author Last, First. Year. *Title of Book*. Place: Publisher.

#### Journal Article
Author Last, First. Year. "Title of Article." *Journal Title* volume (issue): page-page. https://doi.org/xxxxx.

## Key Differences from APA

- Chicago uses footnotes (Notes-Bibliography) or parenthetical citations (Author-Date)
- Chicago uses "and" not "&" in citations
- Chicago capitalises all major words in titles (title case)
- Chicago places publication details differently in notes vs. bibliography
