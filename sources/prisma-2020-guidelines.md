## Purpose

Reference document for the PRISMA 2020 reporting standard. Used when assessing or designing systematic review methodologies.

## Key Requirements

### Title
- Identify the report as a systematic review, meta-analysis, or both

### Abstract
- Structured abstract: background, objectives, data sources, study eligibility criteria, participants and interventions, study appraisal and synthesis methods, results, limitations, conclusions, registration number

### Introduction
- Rationale for the review in the context of existing knowledge
- Explicit statement of the review question(s) using PICO or similar framework

### Methods
- Protocol registration (e.g., PROSPERO)
- Eligibility criteria (inclusion and exclusion)
- Information sources (databases, registers, other sources)
- Search strategy (full electronic search for at least one database)
- Selection process (screening, eligibility assessment)
- Data collection process
- Data items (outcomes, variables)
- Risk of bias assessment (tool used, e.g., RoB 2, ROBINS-I)
- Synthesis methods (quantitative, narrative, or both)

### Results
- Study selection (PRISMA flow diagram)
- Study characteristics
- Risk of bias in individual studies
- Results of syntheses
- Reporting biases
- Certainty of evidence

### Discussion
- Interpretation of findings
- Limitations of the review
- Implications
