## In-Text Citations

### Single Author
- Parenthetical: *(Smith, 2023)*
- Narrative: *Smith (2023) found that…*

### Two Authors
- Parenthetical: *(Smith & Jones, 2023)*
- Narrative: *Smith and Jones (2023) argued…*
- Use "&" inside parentheses; use "and" in narrative text.

### Three or More Authors
- Use the first author followed by "et al." for every citation, including the first.
- Parenthetical: *(Smith et al., 2023)*
- Narrative: *Smith et al. (2023) demonstrated…*

### Direct Quotes — Short (Fewer Than 40 Words)
- Embed within the text in double quotation marks, followed by author, year, and page number.
- *The researchers noted that "memory consolidation occurs primarily during slow-wave sleep" (Smith et al., 2023, p. 47).*
- Narrative form: *Smith et al. (2023) observed that "memory consolidation occurs primarily during slow-wave sleep" (p. 47).*

### Direct Quotes — Block (40 Words or More)
- Start the quote on a new line, indented 1.27 cm (0.5 in) from the left margin.
- Do not use quotation marks around block quotes.
- Place the parenthetical citation after the closing full stop.

> Memory consolidation is a multistage process that unfolds over hours and days following initial encoding. The role of sleep, particularly slow-wave sleep, has been demonstrated across numerous experimental paradigms using both behavioural and neuroimaging measures. (Smith et al., 2023, p. 48)

### Multiple Sources in One Citation
- List alphabetically by first author's surname, separated by semicolons.
- *(Brown, 2021; Jones & Patel, 2022; Smith et al., 2023)*

### Secondary Sources
- When citing a source you found in another source (not read directly), cite the secondary source.
- *Vygotsky's theory (as cited in Smith, 2023) suggests…*
- Only the secondary source (Smith, 2023) appears in the reference list.

## Reference List Formats

### General Principles
- Alphabetical order by first author's surname.
- Hanging indent: first line flush left, subsequent lines indented 1.27 cm (0.5 in).
- Include DOIs wherever available, formatted as a full URL: `https://doi.org/xxxxx`
- For online sources without a DOI, include the URL. Do not add a full stop after a URL or DOI.

### Journal Article
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of the article in sentence case. *Title of the Journal in Title Case and Italics*, *Volume*(Issue), page–page. `https://doi.org/xxxxx`

> Smith, J. R., & Jones, L. M. (2023). The effects of sleep deprivation on working memory performance. *Journal of Cognitive Psychology*, *45*(3), 112–128. `https://doi.org/10.1234/jcp.2023.5678`

### Book
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of the book in sentence case and italics* (Edition, if not the first). Publisher.

> Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, fast and slow*. Penguin Books.

### Edited Book Chapter
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of the chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of the book* (pp. page–page). Publisher. `https://doi.org/xxxxx`

> Chen, W. (2022). Cognitive load in multimedia learning. In R. Mayer & L. Fiorella (Eds.), *The Cambridge handbook of multimedia learning* (3rd ed., pp. 89–106). Cambridge University Press.

### Website
Author or Organisation. (Year, Month Day). *Title of the page or article*. Site Name. URL

> World Health Organisation. (2024, March 12). *Mental health in the workplace*. `https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/mental-health-at-work`

- If the author and site name are the same, omit the site name.
- If no date is available, use (n.d.).

### Report
Author or Organisation. (Year). *Title of the report* (Report No. if available). Publisher. URL

> Office for National Statistics. (2023). *Internet access: Households and individuals, Great Britain 2023* (Statistical Bulletin). `https://www.ons.gov.uk/releases/internetaccesshouseholdsandindividuals2023`

## Document Formatting

### Running Head
- Appears in the page header, flush left, on every page.
- Shortened version of the title, all capitals, maximum 50 characters.
- Student papers: running head is not required unless the instructor requests it.

### Page Numbers
- Flush right in the page header, starting from the title page (page 1).
- Use Arabic numerals throughout.

### Font and Spacing
- Acceptable fonts: Times New Roman 12 pt, Calibri 11 pt, Arial 11 pt, Georgia 11 pt, or Lucida Sans Unicode 10 pt.
- Double-space the entire document, including the reference list, block quotes, and table/figure notes.
- Margins: 2.54 cm (1 in) on all sides.
- Paragraph indent: 1.27 cm (0.5 in) for the first line of each paragraph. Exception: abstract, block quotes, headings, table/figure titles, and reference list entries use different indentation rules.

### Heading Levels

| Level | Format |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Centred, **Bold**, Title Case |
| 2 | Flush left, **Bold**, Title Case |
| 3 | Flush left, ***Bold Italic***, Title Case |
| 4 | Indented 1.27 cm, **Bold**, Title Case, ending with a full stop. Text begins on the same line. |
| 5 | Indented 1.27 cm, ***Bold Italic***, Title Case, ending with a full stop. Text begins on the same line. |

- Use headings in sequence — do not skip levels (e.g. do not jump from Level 1 to Level 3).
- Most papers use Levels 1–3. Levels 4 and 5 are for complex papers with deeply nested sections.

## Tables and Figures

### Table Format
- **Number:** Table 1, Table 2, etc. (bold, flush left, above the table)
- **Title:** Italicised, in title case, on the line below the number
- **Body:** Keep lines clean; use horizontal rules above and below the header row and at the bottom of the table only. Do not use vertical rules or full grid lines.
- **Notes:** Placed below the table. Three types:
  - *General note:* Applies to the whole table. Begins with *Note.*
  - *Specific note:* Refers to a particular column, row, or cell. Uses superscript lowercase letters (a, b, c).
  - *Probability note:* Reports significance levels. Uses asterisks (*p < .05, **p < .01, ***p < .001).

### Figure Format
- **Number:** Figure 1, Figure 2, etc. (bold, flush left, above the figure)
- **Title:** Italicised, in title case, on the line below the number
- **Image:** Clear, high-resolution, legible when printed in greyscale
- **Legend:** If needed, placed within the figure (not as a separate note)
- **Notes:** Same format as table notes, placed below the figure

### Labelling Conventions
- Refer to every table and figure by its number in the text (e.g. "As shown in Table 3…"). Never refer to them as "the table above" or "the figure below."
- Number tables and figures in the order they are first mentioned in the text.
- Tables and figures should appear after their first mention, as close to the relevant text as possible.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using "et al." too early.** For works with one or two authors, always write out all names. "Et al." is only for three or more authors.
2. **Forgetting the comma before the year.** It is *(Smith, 2023)*, not *(Smith 2023)*.
3. **Putting a full stop after a DOI or URL.** The 7th edition explicitly states: do not add a full stop after a hyperlink, as it may interfere with link functionality.
4. **Inconsistent capitalisation in references.** Journal titles use title case and are italicised. Article and book titles use sentence case (only the first word, first word after a colon, and proper nouns are capitalised).
5. **Using "and" inside parenthetical citations.** Use "&" inside parentheses: *(Smith & Jones, 2023)*. Use "and" in narrative form: *Smith and Jones (2023)*.
6. **Omitting the page number for direct quotes.** Every direct quote requires a page or paragraph number.
7. **Including sources in the reference list that are not cited in the text (or vice versa).** The reference list and in-text citations must correspond exactly.
8. **Using outdated running head format.** The 7th edition no longer requires the label "Running head:" on the title page. Simply use the shortened title in capitals.
9. **Single-spacing block quotes or reference lists.** The entire document is double-spaced, with no exceptions.
10. **Using first names in citations.** In-text citations use surnames only. First initials appear only in the reference list.
